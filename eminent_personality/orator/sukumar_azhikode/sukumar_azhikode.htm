sukumar azhikode

azhikode becomes a memory

sukumar azhikode, the legendary literary perzonality, social activist and orator passed away in a private hospital in thrissur. he was 85.his was a towering presence for about six decades in kerala's literary and social spheres through his interventions asan authoritative, scholarly and charismatic orator and versatile writer. his inspiring orations had given him direct access to the masses at all levels. he had an amazing commands over sanskrit, malayalam,and english and his knowledge of indian philosophy and mythology was deep. he had made extensive explorations into western philosophies.

his masterpiece Tathvamasi(malayalam) published in 1984, an authoritative book on indian philosophy, Vedas and Upanishads, won twelve awards including the Central Sahithya Akadami Awards, Kerala Sahithya Akademi Award and the Rajaji Award. he had won the ezhuthachan puraskaram-the highest literary award instituted by the state government in 2004. in jan 2007, he refused to accept the padmashree conferred on him stating that such honours are against the spirit of our constitution which insists on equality of all citizens. azhikode, who begun his career as a teacher had later become the provice chancellor of calicut university. he was also the chairman of the national book trust between 1993 ands 1996.

malayala sahithya patanangal,Vayanayude swargattil, Mahatmavinte margam, Aasante seetakavyam, Malayala sahithya vimarshanam, Mahakavi ulloor, Pathukal Kazhchakal, Guruvinte dukham, and Azhikodinte prabhashanangal also include among his notable works.prominent personalities from all walks of life including defence minister A.K.Antony, chief minister Oommen chandy,leader of opposition VS Achutahnandan, super stars like Mammootty, Mohanlal, writers T.Padmanabhan,ONV Kurup,MT Vasudevan nair, community leaders like Vellappilli Nateshan and Bishop Andrews Thanzhath had him in hospital, days earlier. the mortal remains were consigned to flames with full stste honors at payyambalam in kannur. thousands of people from all walks of life came in droves to pay homage to azhikode at the kannur town square.

Sukumar Azhikode (Malayalam: സുകുമാർ അഴീക്കോട്) is a writer, critic and orator, acknowledged for his contributions to Malayalam and insights on Indian philosophy.[1] He is a bachelor and lives in Eravimangalam near Thrissur. Dr. Sukumar Azhikode (born on May 26, 1926) is a writer, critic and orator, acknowledged for his contributions to Malayalam literary criticism and insights on Indian philosophy. He is a bachelor and lives in Viyyoor near Thrissur. Sukumar Azhikode completed BCom in 1946. He then took BT in Malayalam followed by MA and PhD in Malayalam. Azhikode was a teacher at Rajas High School, Chirackal, St. Aloysius College, Mangalore and Devagiri College, Kozhikode and later Principal of SNM Training College, Moottakunnam, and a Professor and Pro-Vice-Chancellor of Calicut University. He retired in 1986. Azhikode's most famous work is Tatvamasi (1984, Malayalam), an authoritative book on Indian Philosophy, Vedas and Upanishads. Thathvamasi has won twelve awards including the Indian Sahitya Academi Award, Kerala Sahithya Academy Award, Vayalar Award and the Rajaji Award. In Jan 2007, he refused to accept the Padma Shri conferred on him stating that "such honours are against the Constitution. The Constitution says everyone should be treated as equal. Giving such honours at different levels, the State discriminates between people. I see the Padma Shri conferred on me as an opportunity to expose this discrimination." Azhikode is the founder and President of Navabharathavedi – a socio-cultural organization. He has also worked at several newspapers including Varthamanam, Dinabandhu, Malayala Harijan, Desamitram, Navayugam and Dinaprabha. He was the chairman of the National Book Trust between 1993 and 1996. Prof. Sukumar Azhikode, has been selected for the CN Ahmad Moulavi MSS Endowment Award for the year 2002. The award instituted by the Muslim Service Society in memory of the famous Qur'an commentator and reformist the late CN Ahmad Moulavi, goes for his contributions in the cultural field of Kerala and his relentless struggle for the cause of communal harmony and other human values.
