59th Nehru Trophy Boat Race on 13th August 2011 Saturday

The Nehru Trophy Boat Race on the Punnamda Lake, near Alappuzha, held on the second Saturday of August every year, is the most competitive and popular of the boat races. On the day of this fiercely fought boat race, the tranquil lake front is transformed into a sea of humanity with an estimated two lakh people, including tourists from abroad, coming to watch the event. For the people of each village in Kuttanad, a victory at this race for their village boat is something to be celebrated for months to come.

Nehru trophy boat race 2011 awakening song

For the first time in its 59-year-old history, the internationally acclaimed Nehru Trophy Boat Race (NTBR) will have an ‘awakening’ song for itself.

The song, penned on the lines of the existing ‘vanchipattu’ genre by noted dramatist, director and lyricist Kavalam Narayana Panicker, is set to be publicised in a major way ahead of the race, the 59th edition of which is scheduled to be held in Alappuzha on August 13. The recording of the song will be launched by Transport Minister V.S. Shivakumar at the Mithra Studios, Thiruvananthapuram, on Monday.

According to officials of the NTBR publicity committee, Mr. Panicker wrote the song, known as ‘unarthupattu’ in Malayalam, following a request from the committee. The lyricist’s son and noted singer Kavalam Sreekumar, along with Satheesh Thuruthi, Anil Pazhaveedu and singers from the Sopanam Natyagriham will render the verses, after which a CD of the song will be released at Alappuzha later this week.

According to committee convener N. Sunil Kumar, plans were afoot to record and link video clippings from the famed boat race and those of Alappuzha into a montage. This would be done with the help of the Information and Public Relations Department’s Electronic Media wing.

The song and the visual version of the same, Mr. Sunil Kumar said, would be presented on various venues where different cultural programmes prior to the NTBR would be hosted. A special request would also be made to the NTBR Society to make the ‘unarthupattu’ the official welcome song of the race, apart from making it available on the NTBR website.
