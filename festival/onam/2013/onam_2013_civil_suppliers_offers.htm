mobile onam markets to be launched in kerala

Kerala food and civil supplies minister Anoop Jacob said on Friday that the Civil Supplies Corporation would start ten mobile Onam markets in the state.The Government was planning to implement the project in all taluks in the state from the next festive season.The market intervention by the civil supplies corporation had helped to curb the rising prices of commodities to some extent and the intervention of the 'Horticorp' also helped to reduce the prices of vegetables in the festive season.SupplyCo would provide free outlets to sell vegetables procured by Horticorp.

Every district has been allotted two mobile Onam markets each. They will have all items that are available at Maveli stores. While deploying mobile units, preference will be given to areas without Supplyco outlets. The route of operation of mobile markets will be publicised in advance through the media. The mobile markets will have a halt of 30 minutes at each stop.

Kerala's traditional handloom garments- golden jerried sarees, jerried set dhotis, double dhotis - as well as modern (handloom) designer cotton sarees and dhotis, and bed sheets, mosquito nets have all been showcased at the 130 Hantex showrooms in the state. The expo also has readymade shirts and other garments. Employees of semi-government and local self-government bodies, public sector, banks, registered companies and cooperative societies will be given the option to buy Hantex products worth up to Rs 5,000 on five equal monthly installments. There is a 20% rebate on Hantex products in the fair, which will remain open till August 27. Exhibition stalls will be opened in all district headquarters and all the outlets will function on all days, including holidays, from 10am to 8pm.

Horticorp Onam fair

Horticorp started Onam fair in all districts in the state from the first week of August and sell vegetables through special Onam stalls set up with the support of Supplyco. In Trivandrum, four mobile vegetable stalls have been rolled out. In addition to this, four more stalls will come up in the capital city while in Ernakulam four mobile vegetable stalls would be rolled out. In Kozhikode and Kannur, two mobile units and in Kottayam one mobile unit would be rolled out soon.
