here is the nineteenth anniversay of the demolition of babri masjid,nineteen years go on the black day of december 6, 1992, the fear and anguish ... posted by admin on december 6, 2011.


dec 6: ayodhya and faizabad on tuesday will observe the 19th anniversary of babri mosque demolition. the security has been tightened in the twin towns.this year's anniversary has coincided with muharram's 10th day, which is considered as a day of mourning by a section of muslims. nineteen years go on the black day of december 6, 1992, the entire indian muslim community, the many helpless, impoverished and the few well to do muslims, watched helplessly and in abject fear as the ancient babri masjid in ayodhya was demolished, brick by brick by the extremist hindus, egged on by their nationally known leaders. at the same time throughout the wide expanse of india from assam to gujarat and from kashmir to kerala, ordinary muslims were publicly humiliated in countless anti-muslim riots. that day the brutal enemies of indian muslims openly taunted them with slogans like: in countless cities in the nation the houses, shops, schools, businesses of muslims were pillaged and burnt down to ground. any muslims in obvious muslim dresses (tahmad, topi, beard, burqa etc) were waylaid and lynched publicly. for several weeks the lives of muslims were cheap.

on that fateful day the prime minister of india made a solemn pledge in the indian parliament that the indian government will rebuild babri mosque and will bring the perpetrators of the carnage to justice. but today nineteen years later the marauders whom not only the indian media has identified, but also the liberhan commission has identified and has recommended that they be tried in a court of law, are roaming free and crowing. the sovreign indian government has refused to honor its pledge to restore the babri masjid.
