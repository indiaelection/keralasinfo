maharashtra gandhi smarak nidhi

maharashtra gandhi smarak nidhi, located far west in the suburb of kothrud is the oldest landmark in this area built in 1948, spread over 10 acres of land including the Industrial Estate within its premises. the trust is registered under bombay public trust act. maharashtra gandhi smarak nidhi (MGS Nidhi) boasts of close to 40,000 books and 1700 members making its library second largest in pune district.the trust also has a collection of many rare photographs of our Freedom Struggle along with handwritten letters from gandhiji himself. It’s private collection has a rare black and white video footage of more than 5½ hours of Gandhiji along with other video and photo collection.

converted to a trust on 14th Oct 1962, MGS nidhi was formed right after the assassination of mahatma gandhi when stalwarts of indian freedom struggle like sardar vallabhbhai patel, prime minister jawaharlal nehru and others met in vardha to carry forward gandhiji’s 16 point programme of building a modern nation. It was a time when state of maharashtra only had 12 districts and vidharbha and marathwada were not yet part of its borders.

the land itself was purchased for Rs 10,000/- from Mr wandrekar and out of 10 acres & 12r (gunthas), only 3 acres were built in form of gandhi bhavan and the industrial estate. out of the total land, 12 r (gunthas) or 0.3 acre land was gifted by wandrekar to MGS nidhi when jawaharlan nehru visited and could not find a good parking place for his vehicle. over the years, the trust has organized and hosted many rural cleanliness schemes like rural toilets programme, malapatra sansodhan kendra, and hosting stalls in events like yashada. There are 2 more branches of MGS nidhi located in the city of aurangabad and nagpur
