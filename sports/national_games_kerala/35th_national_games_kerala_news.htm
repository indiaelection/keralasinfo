central team in state to oversee preparations

 THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: The Central team visiting Kerala to oversee the preparations for the 35th National Games here, has expressed apprehensions about the work done at some of the venues, while saying that the overall progress of the work was going as per schedule. The Central team under the leadership of Central sports joint secretary Rahul Bhatnagar, Sports Authority of India (SAI) secretary Gopal Krishna, deputy advisor Planning Commission (education and sports) Raman Singh visited Pirappancode swimming pool, Jimmy George indoor stadium and University Stadium. Kerala Olympic Association president M M Abdurehman and LNCPE principal and SAI (Kerala Region) director, G Kishore accompanied the team. “The first day of our visit was fruitful. The work in the two stadiums (University and Jimmy George) and the swimming pool in Pirappancode is progressing steadily. They had prioritised the works into three and we expect it to finish on time,” Rahul Bhatnagar told Express. The team was taken aback when it learned about how Rs one-and-a-half crore was wasted for the renovation of the Water Works’ swimming pool beside the Jimmy George Stadium.The State Government had started constructing a new pool here ignoring the fact that a water supply pipeline ran beneath it. The work has been suspended now.

dates for 35th National Games

Kerala on Friday proposed December 12, 2012 as the date for starting the next edition of the National Games. "We have proposed the auspicious date of 12-12-12 for the 35th National Games. But it depends on the overall construction work going around in stadium and velodrome," Kerala Olympic Association president MM Abdul Rahiman told reporters on Friday.
