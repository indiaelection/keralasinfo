games history

kerala hosted the 27th national games in 1987and is the proud host for the forthcoming 35th national games scheduled for 12.12.2012.government of kerala and kerala olympic association requested the indian olympic association to permit them to host the national games in kerala in response to application dated 15th march 2008.

 the history of kerala is shrouded in legends. the myth that parusarama reclaimed the land called kerala from the sea by throwing his mace may suggest that several parts of the land were made habitable by the receding sea. there are no records to show who the rulers of kerala were before the christian era. it is believed that the ‘ayi’ kingdom flourished in the south in the early centuries of the christian era and the chera kingdom in central kerala in later centuries. with the coming of europeans, kerala society, which had already imbibed several external influences, began transforming into the present modern, cosmopolitan society.

It is for the second time national games is coming to kerala. the first occasion was in 1987 when the 27th national games was held at thiruvananthapuram, kozhikode, alappuzha, kochi, thrissur and kollam. the games is expected to be held at the end of year 2012 in seven districts across the state of kerala namely thiruvananthapuram, kollam, alappuzha, kochi, thrissur, kozhikode and kannur. there will be 36 medal events and two demonstration events.
